## What Coop Is

Coop is a browser-first, local-first knowledge commons for groups that already have useful context
but keep losing it to tabs, chats, notes, and one-off documents. It helps a community gather
signal, shape it into something legible, and decide together what should become shared memory.

The product is intentionally split across two surfaces:

- the browser extension, which is where members launch coops, review drafts, publish, and handle
  bounded operator work
- the companion app (the receiver), which lets people capture audio, photos, files, and links from
  a phone or secondary device

Within the extension, the current roles are:

- `Popup` for quick capture and quick review
- `Chickens` for working candidates and drafts
- `Coops` for shared coop state, archive, and proof
- `Roost` for Green Goods member actions
- `Nest` for membership, operator controls, and settings

## The Core Loop

1. **Capture** context from wherever it already exists — round up your Loose Chickens.
2. **Refine** it locally in the browser before anyone else sees it.
3. **Review** what matters in the popup and Chickens before anything becomes shared.
4. **Share** selected knowledge into the Coop Feed and archive what should last.

That loop is the center of the product. Coop is not trying to replace every tool a community uses.
It is trying to give scattered context a durable path into shared understanding.

The older product story still uses **the Roost** as the metaphor for human judgment. In the current
sidepanel UI, though, the `Roost` tab is the Green Goods member workspace rather than the main draft
review queue.

## What Makes It Different

- Coop defaults to local-first work. Raw captures stay on the device until a person chooses to
  publish.
- The AI story is human-in-the-loop. The browser can structure, score, and draft, but it does not
  silently push shared state on its own.
- Identity is passkey-first. Members do not need to start with a wallet extension just to join a
  coop.
- Shared memory has a durable path. Published artifacts can be synced peer-to-peer and archived
  with verifiable receipts.
- Green Goods is bounded. Members can submit work when a coop uses it, while trusted operators
  handle approvals, assessments, and Hypercert or Karma GAP packaging later.
