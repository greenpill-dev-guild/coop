# Pricing

Coop does not have a public pricing table yet.

## What Exists Today

The repo and docs describe a product that is still in active buildout. In practical terms, access
today looks like one of these paths:

- local development from the open repository
- trusted-tester extension installs outside the Chrome Web Store flow
- guided demo and deployment setups while the product surface is still stabilizing

## What This Means For A Community

If you are evaluating Coop right now, the honest expectation is:

- there is not a self-serve commercial pricing page to compare
- some flows are oriented around builders, testers, and early operators
- live archive and onchain paths depend on environment setup and credentials

## What We Are Not Claiming

This page intentionally does not invent:

- paid tiers
- seat pricing
- enterprise plans
- public SLAs that are not actually documented elsewhere in the repo

## Monetization Direction (Provisional)

The following is a directional outline, not a committed pricing model. It reflects current thinking
and may change as the product matures.

**Core loop stays free.** Capture, local AI synthesis, review, and publishing within a coop will
remain free for individuals and small groups.

**Paid value accrues at the coordination layer:**

- **Archive permanence**: Filecoin-anchored snapshots beyond a free tier could carry a per-snapshot
  or per-GB cost, covering Storacha storage fees.
- **Operator capabilities**: Trusted-node features (scheduled capture, background sync, advanced
  session-key management) could be part of an operator subscription.
- **Community coop hosting**: Larger coops with many members, persistent sync, and shared board
  hosting could introduce seat-based or coop-based pricing.
- **Integrations and connectors**: Premium skill packs, third-party integrations, or advanced
  knowledge graph features could be offered as add-ons.

**Open-source commitment.** The core codebase remains open. Paid features will layer on top of the
open protocol, not gate access to the fundamental capture-refine-share loop.

> **Provisional framing.** The [Durgadas Coordination Structural Integrity Suite](https://github.com/durgadasji/standards)
> provides supporting rationale for why coordination infrastructure should have transparent value
> capture that aligns operator incentives with community outcomes. This framing is provisional and
> will be revisited as the framework stabilizes externally.

## The Practical Read

Today, Coop is best understood as an actively developed coordination product with an open codebase,
an early-access installation posture, and a growing public docs surface.

If that is the stage you want, the best next pages are [How It Works](/how-it-works),
[Creating A Coop](/creating-a-coop), and [Builder Getting Started](/builder/getting-started).
