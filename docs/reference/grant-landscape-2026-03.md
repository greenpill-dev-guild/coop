# Coop Grant Framing Snapshot

<DocMeta />

Original research date: March 29, 2026

This page is now an internal grant-framing snapshot, not a current opportunity matrix. The earlier
version named specific programs, deadlines, amounts, rankings, and action order. Those claims were
date-sensitive and must not be treated as current without a fresh official-source review.

Use this page for reusable Coop positioning and readiness gaps. Before drafting any external
application, create a new official-source research note for the specific funder, date, eligibility
rules, license requirements, amount, deadline, and application path.

For release posture and what Coop can safely claim right now, see
[Current Release Status](/reference/current-release-status). For Ethereum mission alignment, see
[Ethereum Foundation Mandate](/reference/ethereum-foundation-mandate).

## Funding Thesis Memo

### 1. Local-first community memory

Use this framing for open internet, digital commons, sovereignty, and portability-oriented funders.

Core line:

> Coop is browser-first, local-first software that helps communities turn scattered tabs, notes,
> voice, photos, files, and links into reviewed shared memory without defaulting to centralized
> cloud AI or surveillance platforms.

What to emphasize:

- explicit local-first storage and sync
- human review before publish
- passkey-first identity
- CRDT sync, portability, and walkaway properties

What not to overclaim:

- do not lead with "social network"
- do not lead with token incentives
- do not frame Coop as a generic productivity app
- do not imply live onchain or archive rails are public-launch defaults

### 2. Grant and evidence packaging

Use this framing for application-layer, community-operations, grant-ops, and coordination funders.

Core line:

> Coop helps research, grant, and community teams gather evidence across many sources, refine it
> locally, and turn the strongest signals into reviewed briefs, next steps, and durable shared
> context.

What to emphasize:

- browser capture as the intake membrane
- local AI refinement as assistive, not autonomous
- reviewed outputs instead of raw dumps
- better continuity across community funding and coordination work

What not to overclaim:

- do not claim automated grant writing or guaranteed funding outcomes
- do not present the current live onchain rails as the default user path
- do not use stale funder deadlines or amounts from older research

### 3. Verifiable archive plus public-goods rails

Use this framing only when the application can clearly separate mock-first product readiness from
operator-controlled live rails.

Core line:

> Coop pairs local-first collaboration with optional verifiable archive, export, and smart-account
> coordination rails so groups can preserve evidence, prove provenance, and coordinate actions
> without making centralized platforms the system of record.

What to emphasize:

- explicit archive action and receipt/provenance flow
- Safe plus passkey design
- privacy and explicit publish boundaries
- public-goods value beyond a single app

What not to overclaim:

- do not claim live Filecoin anchoring unless live archive mode has been configured and proved
- do not make Safe, session-key, or registry rails sound like public-build defaults
- do not make AI the main identity of the product

### 4. Local AI as support, not theater

AI should be a secondary amplifier, not the main thesis.

Best line:

> Coop uses local-first AI for refinement, speech and multimodal intake, and coordination support;
> it is not a frontier-model wrapper or "AI agent" product in search of a use case.

What to emphasize:

- source-backed current provider wording from [Agent Harness](/reference/agent-harness)
- human review before publish or high-stakes execution
- local model asset and runtime capability caveats

What not to overclaim:

- do not promise perfect extraction or classification
- do not imply capture content is sent to cloud LLMs
- do not imply offline inference works before required model assets are present

## Current Repo Posture That Affects Grants

Strengths:

- the core browser-first and local-first product story is real
- local AI refinement exists in the repo and product model
- archive, privacy, passkey, and smart-account modules are present
- the product is documentable and demoable in a mock-first posture

Gaps that will materially affect applications:

- there is no visible top-level `LICENSE` file in the repo today
- reusable ecosystem-infrastructure asks need a clearer extraction boundary than "fund the app"
- live archive, Safe, Green Goods, session-key, and registry rails require operator configuration and
  should not be sold as public-launch defaults
- most external programs will want public traction, pilot evidence, or official validation that this
  snapshot does not provide

## Application Prep Checklist

Before using this material externally:

- refresh the target funder against official sources on the day of drafting
- record the exact program URL, deadline, eligibility, license terms, amount range, and review path
- decide whether the ask is product, reusable infrastructure, research, or pilot deployment
- make the mock-first versus live-rails boundary explicit
- link only to validation evidence that has been rerun on the current tree
- confirm whether the requested scope needs a top-level license or component-level dual licensing

Core assets that should stay reusable across applications:

- one-page product thesis
- current release posture paragraph
- architecture diagram covering browser capture, local storage/sync, local AI refinement, review,
  publish, optional archive, and optional onchain rails
- validation evidence table with dates and commands
- pilot or user-evidence summary when available

## Refresh Protocol For Future Grant Research

Create a new dated note instead of editing this snapshot into a live matrix. Each opportunity should
include:

- official source URL and retrieval date
- eligibility and licensing constraints
- current deadline or cadence
- amount range only when the official source states it
- exact Coop scope being proposed
- proof needed before submission
- explicit "do not claim" boundaries

Do not reuse stale deadlines, rankings, or probability scores from older research. If the official
source cannot be checked, mark the opportunity as unverified rather than filling the gap with memory.
